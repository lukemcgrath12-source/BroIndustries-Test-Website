Privacy Policy

Effective date: 1 April 2026

BRO Industries Pty Ltd ("BRO Industries", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website at broindustries.com.au or contact us about our plumbing services.

We handle all personal information in accordance with the Australian Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

Information we collect

We may collect the following personal information when you contact us or submit an enquiry:

  • Full name
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Details about your plumbing job or service request
  • Property address (where provided for quoting purposes)

We also collect non-personal information automatically when you visit our website, including browser type, pages visited, and approximate location. This is collected via analytics tools (such as Google Analytics) and is used only to improve our website.

How we use your information

We use the personal information you provide to:

  • Respond to your enquiry or quote request
  • Schedule and carry out plumbing services
  • Send you relevant follow-up communications about your job
  • Improve our services and website

We do not use your personal information for unsolicited marketing without your consent. We will never sell or rent your personal information to third parties.

How we store and protect your information

Your information is stored securely and accessed only by Jack McGrath and John McGrath of BRO Industries for the purpose of delivering our services. We take reasonable steps to protect your information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access.

Enquiries submitted through our website contact form are sent directly to our business email and are not stored on third-party servers beyond standard email infrastructure.

Disclosure to third parties

We do not disclose your personal information to third parties except in the following limited circumstances:

  • Where required by law or a court order
  • Where you have provided explicit consent
  • To advertising platforms (such as Google and Meta) in anonymised or aggregated form for campaign measurement — never in a way that personally identifies you

Cookies and tracking

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies to analyse site traffic and measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns. You can disable cookies in your browser settings, though this may affect website functionality.

We may use Google Analytics and Meta Pixel to understand how visitors interact with our website. These tools collect anonymous usage data. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking via Google's opt-out tool.

Access and correction

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, and to ask us to correct any inaccurate information. To make a request, contact us using the details below. We will respond within a reasonable timeframe.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with a revised effective date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
